Executive Summary

What is VENUE?

VENUE is a Miami-exclusive, concierge-driven event venue marketplace that connects event planners, hosts, and corporate teams with curated local venues. We solve a critical problem: planning a perfect event in Miami is fragmented, opaque, and often mediocre.

Unlike Peerspace (automated, low-touch, 2.6/5 rating) and EventUp (subscription model), VENUE combines:

  • Curation: 31 hand-picked venues across Miami's best neighborhoods (Wynwood, Brickell, Design District, Coral Gables, etc.)
  • Concierge service: Georg personally triages every lead, matches customers to venues, and handles logistics within 2 hours
  • Bilingual expertise: English + Spanish — Miami's competitive advantage
  • Guided wizard: 3-step booking flow designed for first-time event planners (Event Type → Location/Details → Preferences → Results)

Current Status

Prototype complete and deployed. 44 static pages (homepage + 31 venue pages + 9 neighborhood pages + sitemap/robots). Next.js 16 + React 19 + TypeScript. Domain TBD pending Frank partnership formalization. Waiting on: PostHog account, GA4 property, Supabase setup, Google Search Console submission.

The Journey in Detail

Traffic Acquisition

Prospects arrive via Google Ads (high-intent), Instagram (brand awareness), organic search (long-tail), or referrals (planner networks). Conversion rates vary: Google ~3–5%, Instagram ~0.5–1%, referral ~10%+.

Landing & Wizard

Customer lands on homepage, clicks "Find My Venue", enters the 3-step wizard (Event Type → Location/Details → Preferences). Progressive disclosure reduces cognitive load. Premium design builds trust.

Lead Submission

Customer completes form (name, email, phone, event date, guest count, budget, special requests). Form POSTs to FormSubmit.co → Georg's inbox.

Concierge Triage (Critical)

Within 2 hours: Georg reads lead, assesses event type/budget/capacity, matches 2–3 venues from database. Sends Template 1 (acknowledgment) to customer.

Venue Inquiry

Georg sends Template 2 (venue inquiry email) to matched venues. Includes customer event details, preferred dates, expected guest count, budget.

Venue Response (24–48h)

Venue replies with availability, pricing, package options. If no response in 48h, Georg sends Template 6 (follow-up). Tracks outcome.

Customer Notification

Georg sends Template 3 to customer: "Here are your 2–3 venue options." Includes pricing, capacity, reviews, photos, venue contact info.

Booking & Revenue

Customer contacts venue directly OR goes back through VENUE for booking. Either way: if booking confirmed, Georg invoices venue for 10–15% commission (or venue signs $99/mo featured agreement). Template 4 (booking confirmation) sent to both parties.

Partnership Scaling

After 3+ leads to a venue, Georg sends Template 5 (partnership pitch): "You've gotten great leads from us. Let's formalize: 10–15% commission or $99/mo featured." Converts venues to partners.

Competitive Analysis & Key Insight

Peerspace: The Vulnerable Leader ▼

Market position: Largest player in peer-to-peer venues. Raised $37M, valued at $200M+ (2022). Present in 10+ US cities.

Why it failed: Automated matching + low-touch customer service. 2.6/5 rating reflects poor outcomes. During COVID, they laid off 20% of staff and abandoned customer support. Users report: "No response from venues," "Hidden fees," "Low-quality spaces."

Why we win: Concierge + bilingual + Miami expertise. We personally match customers to venues and guarantee response within 24–48h. We speak Spanish. We know every venue owner. This is anti-scalable, which is exactly our moat early on.

Giggster: Niche Wins ▼

Market position: Film/photography focused. Smaller than Peerspace but profitable. Better rating (4.2/5) because they serve a specific use case well.

Why we win: Giggster is not targeting events (birthdays, showers, corporate). They're niche. We're events-first, which is a bigger TAM and better monetization.

Splacer & Tagvenue: Local Curation Models ▼

Market position: Splacer operates in 5 cities (LA, SF, NYC, Austin, Miami-ish). Tagvenue is wedding-focused, ~16 Miami venues.

Why we win: Both are national players optimizing for scale. We're Miami-only, which means: (1) we can build local relationships, (2) we can speak Spanish, (3) we can optimize for Miami event types (beach parties, Wynwood lofts, Brickell corporate), (4) we can move faster than national platforms.

The Venue Report: Capital-Intensive Directory ▼

Market position: SaaS directory for wedding venues. $1.8M revenue, 11 people. Profitable but slow-growing.

Why we win: They're weddings-only. We're all events (birthdays, corporate, showers, galas). Wider TAM. Also: they built a SaaS platform (capital-heavy). We're marketplace (capital-light, immediate unit economics).

Lessons from Competitors (Cold Start Playbook)

  • Geographic constraint works: Peerspace started in SF, then LA, then NYC. Geographic dominance → network effects → revenue. We're starting Miami-only. Right move.
  • Niche focus wins: Giggster (film), The Venue Report (weddings) both have high ratings because they serve their niche well. We should lean into "events" (not just weddings) + bilingual + Miami.
  • Supply-first: All successful marketplaces (Peerspace, Splacer) recruited venues first, then demand (customers). We have 31 venues. Next: demand generation.
  • Customer service is a moat: Peerspace lost trust because of poor support. We gain trust through concierge + bilingual + local relationships. This is defensible for 12–18 months.
  • Automate later: We start manual (Georg triages leads). When we hit 100+ leads/month, we build CRM/automation. But not before.

Lead Handling Workflow (Step-by-Step)

This is the core operational playbook. Every lead follows this 9-step process. Georg does all of this manually in Months 1–3. We automate after Month 6.

Step 1: Lead Arrives

Customer completes wizard → submits form → FormSubmit POSTs to georg.chimion@gmail.com. Email arrives with: name, email, phone, event type, date, guest count, budget, special requests.

Step 2: Acknowledgment (Within 30 min)

Send Template 1: "Thanks for reaching out! We're matching you with 2–3 perfect venues. You'll hear from us within 2 hours." Sets expectation, builds trust.

Step 3: Triage & Matching (Within 2 hours)

Georg reads lead, assesses: event type, budget, date, guest count. Queries VENUE database for venues that match. Criteria: (1) event type in bestFor array, (2) capacity fits, (3) pricing in budget. Log in Airtable CRM.

Step 4: Venue Inquiry (Within 2.5 hours)

Send Template 2 to each venue: "We have a client for you: [Event Type], [Date], [Guests], [Budget], [Special Requests]. Can you confirm availability and pricing?" Include customer contact info so venue can follow up directly if interested.

Step 5: Wait for Response (24–48 hours)

Venue responds (or doesn't). Update Airtable with venue status: "Pending", "Available", "Booked", "Not Interested". If no response in 48h, send follow-up email.

Step 6: Follow-up if No Response (After 48h)

Send Template 6: "Hey [Venue], just following up on the lead from Step 4. Interested?" If still no response after 2 follow-ups, flag venue for offline conversation (call/text).

Step 7: Customer Notification (After 24–48h of venue responses)

Send Template 3 to customer: "Great news! We found these 2–3 venues for your [Event Type]:" Include: venue name, capacity, pricing, photos, reviews, amenities, venue contact info, booking link (if available). Encourage customer to reach out directly to venues.

Step 8: Booking Tracking (After customer contacts venue)

Customer books venue (directly with venue OR through VENUE if we handle booking). Georg confirms booking in Airtable. If booked: (1) Send Template 4 (confirmation) to customer, (2) Invoice venue for 10–15% commission OR facilitate $99/mo featured agreement, (3) Log outcome in CRM.

Step 9: Partnership Pitch (After 3+ leads)

After 3+ leads sent to a venue, call/email with Template 5 (partnership pitch): "You've gotten quality leads from us. Let's formalize: 10–15% commission on bookings + priority matching. Interested?" Convert venues from ad-hoc to formal partners.
